What is the GuzhengAir

As the name suggests, the GuzhengAir is an electronic instrument designed to simulate the playstyle and unique sound of a traditional Chinese guzheng. To play the GuzhengAir, place a hand or finger (typically the thumb) in front of the main ultrasonic sensor. Then, when the finger is lifted, a sample of a single guzheng note is played (to resemble plucking a string).

The instrument is tuned to the G major pentatonic scale, range D4-B5; and the pitch of the sample is shifted according to the finger's proximity to the sensor before "plucking"- lower notes are played closer to the sensor, whereas higher notes are played closer to the player.

Why make the GuzhengAir

When figuring out what instrument I wanted to create, I considered many ideas. I was inspired by existing gestural instruments, so I decided to create an instrument with its own unique gesture. I set my mind on a plucking motion and realized I could reimagine the guzheng.

Throughout my life, I have pursued many ways of connecting with my Chinese heritage; part of this was discovering a love of traditional Chinese instruments and music. In my life, I have learned many instruments, of both Western and Eastern origin, and I would have loved to learn the guzheng. But there are a number of reasons why I was less successful with the guzheng than other instruments:

  • Guzheng technique is significantly right-hand dominant; I'm left-handed.
  • The guzheng is not easily portable; they are just over 5 feet in length and weigh between 20 and 30 pounds.
  • Guzhengs, like many instruments, are expensive; they range from a few hundred dollars to a couple thousand, not including strings, picks, etc.
In addition to my desire to create a unique gesture, I aim to solve these issues with my design of the GuzhengAir.

Next Steps

As I continue to work on the GuzhengAir, I have many additions and improvements that I want to implement:

  1. Exchange the guzheng sample for a generated signal that resembles a guzheng. This should make the pitch shifting easier and sound more realistic, especially for the higher pitches played on the current model that often get distorted.
  2. Add capabilities to play trills and glissandos - techniques and sounds that are characteristic of the guzheng.
  3. Add the ability to play multiple pitches at once.
  4. Add octave-switching functionality. (The guzheng typically spans 4 octaves, wheras the GuzhengAir currently covers 2 octaves)
I will continue to update this page as these next steps are met.
